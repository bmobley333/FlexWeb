Char Concept
📜 Character Concept Prompt
Task:
 I will provide you with a few sentences to several paragraphs describing a character idea, archetype, or backstory. Using the attached game rules and formatting standards, expand this into a complete character concept chronicle.
Output Structure:
1. Character Sheet (Long Flavor Version)

   * Present as labeled sections with concise but rich detail.

   * Sections (include all, even if sparse):

      * Name: Full name/title.

      * Race: Race and subrace/bloodline if applicable.

      * Class: Archetype or role.

      * Regional & Clan Origins: Geography, culture, history that shaped them.

      * Family & Personal History: Parents, mentors, pivotal figures.

      * Appearance: Height, build, skin, hair, eyes, tattoos/scars/marks.

      * Equipment & Clothing: Signature gear, relics, weapons, items.
      * Magic Items: Reference the rules for categories (Minor, Lesser, Greater, Artifact) and be sure the character has several listed as appropriate for the character concept.

      * Core skill sets: Thievery, sailing, mountaineering, etc. as implied in the text.

      * Powers: These can be general areas such as Healing or Fire Magic, but with narrative examples

      * Personality & Motivation: Attitude, beliefs, goals, vow.

      * Quick Text-Chain Summary: A one-sentence compressed version for quick reference.

         2. Chronicle of [Name], [Title/Archetype]

            * Write in narrative style, like a saga or myth.

            * Suggested substructure:

               * Regional & Clan Origins (mythic/historical voice).

               * Childhood & Shaping Events (personal tone, sensory detail).

               * Training & Mentorship (discipline, trials, quotes from mentors).

               * Marks of Identity (tattoos, relics, vows, scars).

               * Wanderer’s Journey (why they travel, what they seek).

               * Closing Verse (legend-like ending with vow, prophecy, or whispers).

Style Guide:
                  * Rich, immersive, and lore-driven.

                  * Use sensory and cultural detail.

                  * Avoid game mechanics except in Magic Items, Core skill Sets, Powers, where implied abilities should be named clearly for later use in Phase 2.

                  * Keep it consistent with the world’s rules (no unsupported mechanics).

                  * Maintain separation of “flavor” (Phase 1) from “rules & effects” (Phase 2).

Chronicle of Dru’Vash, Shadowfist Healer of the Ashveil
Regional & Clan Origins
The Ashveil Highlands were jagged and unyielding, where ash fell like snow and molten rivers glowed beneath cracked stone. Here, the Stonefang Clan — once fearsome raiders — limped to near extinction. They found unlikely refuge among the Umbral Kin, mortals shaped by living shadow, and from their bond arose new bloodlines. Healing became sacred, for it had delivered them from the edge of oblivion.
________________


Childhood & Shaping Events
Born to Kraza and Reth’Vash, Dru’Vash grew amid caverns lit by firelight and veiled in shadow. He learned silence, balance, and the pulse of volcanic earth. When plague struck, he saw his father kneel with obsidian shard in hand, weaving shadow into warmth. Lives once certain to end endured. That moment seared Dru’Vash’s path — healing as defiance, not mercy.

Training & Mentorship

Marks of Identity
Initiation left him scarred and marked — spirals of ki etched across arms, a rune of survival burned into his shoulder, and a crescent tattoo beneath his eye. These were not adornments but vows, a reminder that shadow and light must walk together. His volcanic shard, relic of his father, remained the anchor of his oath.
________________


Wanderer’s Journey
Leaving the Highlands, Dru’Vash bore little but his shard, his staff, and his vow. He journeys not to conquer but to recover what is lost: healing arts of body and spirit scattered across the world. To those he aids, he is a protector. To enemies, he is the shadow that strikes with force yet leaves renewal in its wake.
________________

Among allies, they say: “When Dru’Vash walks, the shadows heal as well as harm.”
 Among foes, they whisper: “Strike him down quickly, for every breath he takes may raise another to fight.”
 And in his heart the vow beats steady: Strength exists to preserve life, not take it.


Effect Standardization
⚙️ Effect Standardization (Updated)
1) Structure & Format
                                                               * Name: Always clear, evocative, and unique.

                                                               * Effect Statement:

                                                                  * Begin with the mechanical trigger/action (e.g., “On hit…,” “When you Heal…,” “Mind👁️ or target is Stunned”).

                                                                  * Follow with the mechanical outcome (Dmg, Heal, buff, MR👣, AR🛡️, Advantage, reroll, etc.).

                                                                     * Usage:

                                                                        * Standard shorthand only → 1-Rnd, 1-Enc, 2-Enc, 3-Enc, 1-Day, 2-Day, 3-Day, 1-Luck.

                                                                        * Single-use consumables (potions, scrolls, etc.) = 1.
                                                                        * These are all the options. Never invent new Usage.

                                                                           * Action Codes: Always one of AM, A, M, P, F.

                                                                              * Never invent new codes.

________________


2) Effect Wording
                                                                                 * Keep effects succinct and tidy.

                                                                                 * Always begin with words (never a symbol).

                                                                                 * Format examples:

                                                                                    * On hit; Magic✨ fire Dmg

                                                                                    * Create hazard; foes entering sq take Magic✨ Dmg

                                                                                    * Req melee touch; Magic✨ Atk and lightning Dmg

                                                                                    * Raise spikes in 2x2sq; foes entering suffer Magic✨ Atk & Dmg

                                                                                    * Summon whirlwind; Magic✨ Atk pushes foes d4sq

                                                                                    * Choose fire/ice/lightning; Atk & Dmg+1 for 1 Enc

Rules for specific categories
                                                                                       * Use all game emojis as warranted:
                                                                                       * In particular most abilities are based on the four Attributes (Atr): Might 💪 , Motion🏃, Mind 👁️, Magic ✨.  Such as a Stealth🏃skill.
                                                                                       * Other common emojis are: Vit❤️, MR👣, Skill🎓, Power⚡, AR🛡️, Atr✅, Gear🧰, magic item✨. Weapon⚔️, Attack⚔️ , or Block⚔️. 
                                                                                       * Character’s Point of View: Phrase from caster/character perspective (e.g., Nish+2, not “foe Nish-2”). Never word abilities from the monster or victim’s point of view as monsters DO NOT roll. They have fixed Difficulties.

                                                                                       * Healing: Always Heal X or Heal self X (never “regain”).

                                                                                       * Conditions/Debuffs: Always specify governing Atr and outcome.

                                                                                          * Example: Mind👁️ or target is Stunned.

                                                                                             * Buffs/Bonuses:

                                                                                                * Always shorthand: Atk+2 for 1 rnd, Nish+3 for 1 Enc, AR🛡️+1 for 1 rnd.

                                                                                                   * Movement: Anchor to MR👣 or sq.

                                                                                                      * Example: Move double MR👣 this rnd, Teleport 12sq to visible shadow.

                                                                                                         * Positioning:

                                                                                                            * Example: If Atk hits; push target 3sq.

                                                                                                               * Utility/Exploration:

                                                                                                                  * Example: Stealth🏃 with Advantage, Magic✨ to detect stone weakness.

________________


3) Duration
                                                                                                                     * Default: “for 1 rnd.”

                                                                                                                     * If shorter: say “this rnd.”

                                                                                                                     * If longer: specify for 1 Enc or for the Day.
                                                                                                                     * Do not create custom durations for ability Effects.

________________


4) Rolls & Saves
                                                                                                                        * Always specify governing Atr: Might💪, Motion🏃, Mind👁️, Magic✨.

                                                                                                                        * Most common Defense rolls:

                                                                                                                           * Block (Might💪)

                                                                                                                           * Dodge (Motion🏃)

                                                                                                                           * Resist (Magic✨)

________________


5) Keywords & Consistency
                                                                                                                              * Dmg = Damage

                                                                                                                              * Heal = Heal X / Heal self X

                                                                                                                              * AR🛡️ = AR🛡️+X for 1 rnd

                                                                                                                              * Advantage/Disadvantage = always capitalized and tied to roll type

                                                                                                                              * Teleport vs Move = distinct; teleport ignores terrain

                                                                                                                              * Reroll = Reroll one failed [roll type]

                                                                                                                              * Opportunity attacks = Make 1 opportunity Atk

                                                                                                                              * Areas/Auras: scope as needed (adjacent, nearby, in Xsq, earshot).

________________


6) Magic Items✨
                                                                                                                                 * Effects follow same Effect → Usage → Action format.

                                                                                                                                 * Limited-use info belongs only in Usage, not Effect.

                                                                                                                                 * Example:

                                                                                                                                    * On Touch; Heal self 1

                                                                                                                                                      * 20% Description-only rooms (scenery, lore, no combat/puzzle/treasure)
                                                                                                                                                      * 20% Trap, Puzzle, or Treasure rooms
                                                                                                                                                      * 10% Mixed rooms (any combination above)

                                                                                                                                                         * Description:

                                                                                                                                                            * Each room should have a multi-sentence vivid description.
                                                                                                                                                            * Complex rooms may use a full paragraph.
                                                                                                                                                            * ALL details provided in the rough room descriptions below MUST appear in the room Description. Any comments or questions in parenthesis (e.g. “(Why?)” or “(Trapped?)”). Must be creatively resolved and included.
                                                                                                                                                            * Include elevation notes, lighting, atmosphere, and terrain features.
                                                                                                                                                            * Player-hidden details (GM’s eyes only) should be marked in (🔎).
                                                                                                                                                            * Example: The northwest corner holds a precarious stack of eight crates, one painted with a crude red cross (🔎originally for medicine, now repurposed to store poisons).

                                                                                                                                                               * Specials/Twists:

                                                                                                                                                                  * Every room should have at least one unique twist (environmental hazard, unusual foe tactic, hidden feature, narrative element).

                                                                                                                                                                     * Loot:

                                                                                                                                                                        * ~20% of rooms should include custom loot, particularly referencing character concept items.
                                                                                                                                                                        * List loot in a detailed entry with item name, description, and classification if magical.
                                                                                                                                                                        * Plot:
                                                                                                                                                                        * Each bullet below needs addressed in one to three rooms each as plot element tie ins:
                                                                                                                                                                        * Items, people, etc. hinted at in the previous encounter area
                                                                                                                                                                        * Text, clues, items needed to point at the next encounter area or Act - At least one clue should typically include a paragraph-long text that is either spoken by someone or something or found in a book or scrap of paper, that blatantly points the PCs to the next encounter area or Act (where do the PCs go next and why do they go there).

Combat Room Requirements
                                                                                                                                                                        * Always list monsters in this format:
4 Orc Guards (Heavy Leather, Scimitars, Bucklers) 🚩14, 👣10, ⚔️18/12(2), 🛡️16/2 ❤️12 – [💪18/🏃12/👁️10/✨10] (Poisoned blades Magic✨ or Weakened).

                                                                                                                                                                           * Number & Name (Armor and weapons) = e.g. 4 Orc Guards (Heavy Leather, Scimitars, Bucklers)
                                                                                                                                                                           * 🚩 = Nish
                                                                                                                                                                           * 👣 = MR
                                                                                                                                                                           * ⚔️ = Atk/Dmg(Minimum Dmg)
                                                                                                                                                                           * 🛡️ = Def / AR🛡️
                                                                                                                                                                           * ❤️ = Vit
                                                                                                                                                                           * Brackets = Attribute Scores [💪 / 🏃 / 👁️ / ✨]
                                                                                                                                                                           * Conditions, special abilities, or afflictions in ( ) at the end.

                                                                                                                                                                              * Every monster mentioned must be detailed and complete.

                                                                                                                                                                                 * Do not say “see monster in room X” or “as above.”
                                                                                                                                                                                 * Always restate the full monster stats and abilities.

                                                                                                                                                                                    * Combat scaling:

                                                                                                                                                                                       * Encounters should either use tougher stat blocks or ~1.5x the usual monster count, as players are very strong.
                                                                                                                                                                                       * Encourage mixed forces (elites, fodder, ranged, hidden foes, terrain-based threats).

________________


Trap / Puzzle Room Requirements
                                                                                                                                                                                          * Include where the trap/puzzle is located and how it appears.
                                                                                                                                                                                          * Describe mechanics of operation (trigger, visibility, clues).
                                                                                                                                                                                          * State effect/outcome in rules terms per Effect Standardization.
                                                                                                                                                                                          * Example:
                                                                                                                                                                                          * A 3x3sq concealed floor section in the NW corner collapses into a spiked pit, 15’ deep, walls coated in oil. On trigger: all creatures fall; Motion🏃 or take 15 Dmg + Poisoned (Magic✨ Dif 17).
________________


Mixed Rooms
                                                                                                                                                                                          * Blend combat + trap, or combat + puzzle, combat + treasure, treasure + trap, treasure + puzzle, or trap + puzzle.
                                                                                                                                                                                          * Ensure both aspects are fully fleshed out.
________________


✅ Output Format:
 For each room:
                                                                                                                                                                                          1. Room #: Name
                                                                                                                                                                                          2. Description: Narrative text with sensory detail.
                                                                                                                                                                                          3. Monsters/Traps/Puzzles/Treasure: As required by room type.
                                                                                                                                                                                          4. Special/Twist: Any unique environmental or narrative feature.
                                                                                                                                                                                          5. Loot (if present): Detailed list with standardized effects.

Room 3 – Lieutenant’s Living Room (Ransom Chamber)
Narrative/Description:
 This spacious chamber doubles as a negotiation hall for ransom payments. A massive 20x40ft rug dominates the floor, its weave stained with years of spilled ale and blood. A sturdy wooden table with five mismatched chairs sits in the center. The northwest corner holds a precarious stack of eight crates, one painted with a crude red cross. The southeast is cluttered with barrels, rolled rugs, and crates of spears. A large candelabra burns low on a side table at the south.
Mechanical Notes:
                                                                                                                                                                                          * Encounter/Monsters:

                                                                                                                                                                                             * 1 Orc Lieutenant (Plate, Warhammer) 🚩16        👣8 , ⚔️20 / 12 (2), 🛡️18 / 3, ❤️16, 💪22 / 🏃12 / 👁️12 / ✨12 (Inspires allies)

                                                                                                                                                                                                * Traps/Puzzles: Rug conceals a spiked pit trap (10ft deep, Dmg 10 + Immobilized). Triggered if crate pile is toppled or weight shifts wrong.

Master Oringus is supernatural and will come for you from his Keep of the Vale and avenge us - so win or lose, you will fail.”
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Twist: This is the room most used for “safe” ransom meetings. The candelabra flames burn oddly green when hostages are nearby, tied into necromantic wards from Room 6.

Roll 20 Tips


Laying Out a Map
                                                                                                                                                                                                * First - Be SURE you are on the map layer to drag and drop map (choose <Adjust Page size> - though this is kind of meaningless but doesn’t distort image)
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Always do (for light etc even if no grid)
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Zoom WAY IN
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Regardless if Map has a grid or you want to define one: right click map again and <Advanced> <Align to Grid> select 3x3 choose <Align to Grid> - this will dramatically change image size - that’s ok.
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Next right click on map in Roll20 <Advanced> <Set Dimensions> and write down the width and height in pixels - in maps cog (at top under folder) set exact same pixels.
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Under cog, under folder icon at top of screen
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Turn on grid
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Set pixels width and height to match map
                                                                                                                                                                                                *  set grid color to a striking color.
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Zoom WAY out - now move map to cover page area
                                                                                                                                                                                                * On map <atl> arrow to get alignment “near perfect”
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Can also <alt> drag edges for more perfect alignment
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Under cog, set grid color to invisible
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Click OFF Map (due to accidental move danger) and not Light
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Wall, Doors, Windows, light sources - be SURE LIght layer
                                                                                                                                                                                                * LIGHT layer
                                                                                                                                                                                                * Trick: place doors, windows FIRST - then walls as is easy to start/stop in center of their end circles
